Safety & Side Effects

Honest talk about side effects

GLP-1 medications are generally well-tolerated, but like all prescription medications they do have side effects. Most are gastrointestinal, mild to moderate, and improve significantly within the first few weeks of treatment. Dr. Anthony manages side effects through careful dose titration â€” starting low and increasing gradually.

Nausea

The most common side effect, especially during dose increases. Usually temporary and manageable with eating adjustments.

Decreased appetite

This is both a side effect and the intended mechanism. Dr. Anthony monitors nutrition to ensure you're fueling properly.

Digestive changes

Diarrhea, constipation, or mild abdominal discomfort may occur early in treatment and typically resolve with time.

Injection site reactions

Minor redness or itching at the injection site. Rotating injection locations helps minimize this.

Fatigue or dizziness

Occasionally reported during initial weeks, often related to caloric reduction. Typically resolves as your body adjusts.

Important: GLP-1 medications are not appropriate for everyone. Patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, MEN 2 syndrome, or history of pancreatitis require careful evaluation. Dr. Anthony conducts a thorough medical review before prescribing any medication.
